## Section copy

### 1) Hero
Recommended
- Headline: Solana research and automation infrastructure for trading teams.
- Subheadline: Trader Ralph delivers x402-gated APIs, webhooks, and tool endpoints that technical and operational teams can deploy today.
- Value proof line: Built for API users, quant teams, DAOs, and crypto funds that need clear integration surfaces without self-serve sprawl.
- Primary CTA: Request manual onboarding
- Secondary CTA: Sign in (approved accounts)

Optional variant
- Headline: A Solana-native infrastructure layer for agentic market operations.
- Subheadline: Integrate standalone resources now; expand your operating stack as your workflows mature.

### 2) Problem -> Solution
Recommended
- Problem: Research signals, automation logic, and operational handoffs are often fragmented across scripts and dashboards.
- Solution: Trader Ralph provides one Solana-focused infrastructure layer where APIs, webhooks, and tool endpoints can be consumed independently or combined.

Optional variant
- Problem: Teams spend too much time maintaining glue code and too little time improving decisions.
- Solution: Trader Ralph reduces integration overhead with reusable Solana-native service endpoints.

### 3) Current offerings
Recommended
- APIs: Solana-focused research and execution-support endpoints.
  Example: Route market snapshots into internal scoring or risk checks.
- Webhooks: Event-driven delivery for monitoring and workflow automation.
  Example: Trigger internal alerts and downstream jobs when thresholds are hit.
- Tool endpoints: Standalone machine-consumable resources for internal agents.
  Example: Call one resource from an existing runbook without adopting a full platform migration.

Optional variant
- APIs: Structured Solana market intelligence endpoints.
- Webhooks: Reliable signals for desk-level operations.
- Tools: Composable resources for existing agent stacks.

### 4) x402 model explainer
Recommended
- x402, in plain terms: Your systems request only the resources they need and can consume each API, webhook, or tool endpoint as a standalone service.
- Standalone consumption: Integrate endpoint-by-endpoint into your existing stack.
- Licensing + own-keys flow: Licensing and key handling are configured during manual onboarding based on team requirements.
- Billing/usage pattern: Usage scope and commercial terms are defined during onboarding; self-serve checkout is not active.

Optional variant
- x402 keeps access composable: consume individual resources without bundling into a monolith.

### 5) Built for today vs roadmap
Recommended
- Built for today: Today we provide research and automation infrastructure on Solana.
- Roadmap (clearly marked): Fund custody/capital deployment is a distinct future phase and not yet part of live product functionality.

Optional variant
- Built for today: Production-facing APIs, webhooks, and tooling endpoints.
- Roadmap: Capital operations are planned but not currently offered.

### 6) Why now / why Solana
Recommended
- Solana offers the market activity and execution context needed for continuous, low-latency research and automation loops.
- Teams can integrate incrementally instead of replacing existing internal systems.

Optional variant
- Solana gives operationally relevant throughput and composability for desk-level automation.

### 7) Customer proof / trust
Recommended
- During onboarding, we provide architecture walkthroughs, integration references, and clear operating expectations before access is granted.

Optional variant
- Access is controlled, integrations are reviewed, and operating assumptions are aligned before production use.

### 8) Pricing / access model
Recommended
- Access model: Manual onboarding only.
- Pricing model: Scope and terms are set during onboarding calls; no self-serve tiers are live.

Optional variant
- Commercial terms are matched to integration scope and operating requirements through manual review.

Recommended
